BORDER FORCE OPERATIONAL MANUAL — EXTRACT FOR POLICYASSIST
Version 4.2 — FICTIONAL TRAINING DOCUMENT. Not for operational use.

SECTION 1 — ENTRY REQUIREMENTS: GENERAL PRINCIPLES

1.1 All passengers arriving at the UK border must present a valid travel
document. Acceptable documents are listed in Annex C of this manual.
Officers must verify the document belongs to the holder, is genuine,
and has not expired.

1.2 Where a passenger presents an expired document, the officer must
refer the case to the Duty Higher Officer (DHO). Officers must not
grant entry on an expired document under any circumstances.

1.3 Passengers travelling on the UK Electronic Travel Authorisation
(ETA) scheme must hold a valid ETA linked to the travel document
presented. If the ETA cannot be verified electronically, the officer
should attempt manual verification via the Border Crossing system
before refusing boarding or entry.

1.4 Children under 18 travelling unaccompanied must be referred to a
Safeguarding Lead for a welfare conversation before entry is granted.
This applies regardless of nationality or document type.

SECTION 2 — QUEUES AND PASSENGER FLOW

2.1 The Port Duty Manager is responsible for queue management. When
average queue time at the primary control point exceeds 45 minutes,
the Port Duty Manager must open all available desks and may authorise
the use of ePassport gates for eligible cohorts.

2.2 Officers must not close a staffed desk during a declared surge
period without authorisation from the Port Duty Manager.

2.3 Passengers requiring assistance (mobility, medical, or travelling
with infants) should be directed to the priority lane where one is
available. Where no priority lane exists, officers should process
these passengers at the nearest open desk.

SECTION 3 — REFERRALS AND ESCALATION

3.1 The following cases must always be referred to the DHO:
(a) suspected document fraud;
(b) expired or damaged travel documents;
(c) passengers flagged by the watchlist system;
(d) any case where the officer is not satisfied as to identity;
(e) unaccompanied minors (see 1.4, refer to Safeguarding Lead first).

3.2 Referrals must be logged in the Case Referral System (CRS) within
one hour of the referral being made. The log must include the
passenger reference, the reason code, and the referring officer's ID.

3.3 If the DHO is unavailable for more than 20 minutes, the officer
must contact the Regional Operations Centre for a remote decision.
Passengers must not be left unsupervised in a holding area while a
decision is pending.

SECTION 4 — SHORT-TERM HOLDING

4.1 A passenger may be directed to a short-term holding room only when
a referral decision is pending and the passenger cannot remain at the
control point. The maximum continuous period in a short-term holding
room is 24 hours.

4.2 Passengers in a holding room must be offered water and refreshments
at intervals of no more than 2 hours, and must have access to a
telephone to notify a family member or legal representative.

4.3 A welfare check must be recorded every 30 minutes. The record must
state the time, the officer's ID, and any concerns observed. Welfare
checks for minors must be conducted by an officer with current
safeguarding training.

4.4 Any passenger held for more than 4 hours must be reviewed by the
DHO, and again every 4 hours thereafter. Each review must be recorded
in the CRS.

SECTION 5 — GOODS AND DECLARATIONS

5.1 Passengers carrying goods above their personal allowance must
declare them at the red channel or via the online declaration service.
Officers at the green channel may stop and question any passenger.

5.2 Cash of £10,000 or more (or equivalent) entering or leaving Great
Britain must be declared. Failure to declare may result in seizure of
the cash and a penalty of up to £5,000.

5.3 Prohibited items include controlled drugs, offensive weapons, and
products of animal origin from non-approved countries. Suspected
prohibited items must be detained and logged, and the passenger
referred to a specialist officer.

SECTION 6 — SYSTEM OUTAGES

6.1 During a Border Crossing system outage, officers must revert to
manual processing under Contingency Procedure BC-7. Manual entries
must be reconciled with the system within 48 hours of restoration.

6.2 During an outage, ePassport gates must be closed and all
passengers processed at staffed desks. The Port Duty Manager must
notify the Regional Operations Centre within 15 minutes of the outage
beginning.
